SyncSense: Music Supervisor Agent for AI Video

Music AI Leonardo Production

SyncSense is our “music supervisor agent” that locks emotion, score, and picture into a single controllable loop. Starting from a creative brief, we extract a time-varying valence/arousal curve plus descriptive tags, then force every downstream decision—music selection, harmonic evolution, visual pacing, and editorial beats—to respect that trajectory. The result is AI-generated footage that actually feels right instead of just looking slick.

Pipeline at a Glance

The system runs as five deterministic stages: a prompt or beat sheet becomes the emotion curve; we branch into original cue generation or metadata-aligned retrieval; Leonardo and Canvas handle motif-driven visuals locked to the musical grid; then we ship synchronized masters plus A/B variants that highlight the impact of the right vs wrong score.

Powered by Leonardo

Leonardo lets us establish palettes, recurring motifs, and beat-accurate transitions quickly. Canvas delivers frame-specific in/out-painting, while Alchemy and upscalers finish assets for both widescreen and vertical. Batch rendering makes it affordable to test multiple emotional curves without blowing up the GPU budget.

Toolchain & Ops Discipline

Around that core we keep production basics sharp: Runway/Pika/Luma for motion assists, ControlNet/IP-Adapter for continuity, Udio/Suno for temp cues, and a finishing line that covers Ableton, iZotope RX, Adobe Audition, Premiere, After Effects, Resolve (ACES), Topaz, and Frame.io. Everything lives in a prompt library with seed and energy tracking so the workflow stays reproducible and sustainable.

Deliverables & Timeline

We commit to a working prototype, three 30–60s proof-of-concept shorts with A/B comparisons, a two-minute process film, a public prompt atlas, and a social package. The six-week schedule moves from taxonomy and look-dev to clip production, music-swap testing, and final polish—always prioritizing emotionally coherent edits.
